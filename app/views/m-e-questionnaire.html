Instructions for the MEQ

  • This scale is the original Morningness-Eveningness Questionnaire or MEQ. The MEQ contains 19 items and takes about 5 minutes to complete.
  • Answer each question without considering how you answered a previous question -- your answer to one question does not have to match your answer to another question. Also, if you already completed the 2-item CIRENS Scale, answer the MEQ without considering how you answered the CIRENS items.
  • If none of the available MEQ answers fits you exactly, please pick the answer that is closest.
  • In order to calculate your score, you must answer ALL 19 items. However, if you decide that you do not want to complete the MEQ, click at any time to erase your answers and return to the HOME MENU.

Please answer the following questions to the best of your ability:

  • 1. Considering only your own "feeling best" rhythm, what time would you get up if you were entirely free to plan your day?
  • 2. Considering only your own "feeling best" rhythm, what time would you go to bed if you were entirely free to plan your evening?
  • 3. If there is a specific time that you have to get up in the morning, how dependent are you on being awakened by an alarm clock?
  • 4. How easy do you find getting up in the morning?
  • 5. How alert do you feel during the first half hour after awakening in the mornings?
  • 6. How is your appetite during the first half hour after awakening in the mornings?
  • 7. During the first half hour after awakening in the morning how tired do you feel?
  • 8. When you had no commitments the next day, what time would you go to bed compared to your usual bedtime?
  • 9. You have decided to engage in some physical exercise. A friend suggests that you do this for one hour twice a week, and the best time for him/her is between 7:00-8:00AM. Bearing in mind nothing else but your own "feeling best" rhythm, how do you think you would perform?
  • 10. At what time in the evening do you feel tired and as a result in need of sleep?
  • 11. You want to be at your peak performance for a test that you know is going to be mentally exhausting and lasting for two hours. You are entirely free to plan your day and considering only your own "feeling best" rhythm, which ONE of the four testing times would you choose?
  • 12. If you went to bed at 11:00PM, what would your level of tiredness be?
  • 13. For some reason you have gone to bed several hours later than usual, but there is no need to get up at any particular time the next morning. Which ONE of the following are you most likely to experience?
  • 14. One night you have to remain awake between 4:00-6:00 AM in order to carry out a night watch. You have no time commitments the next day. Which ONE of the following alternatives will suit you best?
  • 15. You have to do two hours of hard physical work. You are entirely free to plan your day and considering only your own "feeling best" rhythm which ONE of the following times would you choose?
  • 16. You have decided to engage in hard physical exercise. A friend suggests that you do this for one hour twice a week and the best time for him/her is between 10:00 PM-11:00 PM. Bearing in mind only your own "feeling best" rhythm, how well do you think you would perform?
  • 17. Suppose that you can choose your own work hours. Assume that you worked a FIVE-hour day (including breaks) and that your job was Interesting and paid by results. Which FIVE CONSECUTIVE HOURS would you select?
  • 18. At what time of day do you think that you reach your "feeling best" peak?
  • 19. One hears about "morning" and "evening" types of people. Which ONE of these types do you consider yourself to be?

    MEQ scores range from 16 to 86:

    The MEQ was originally developed in 1976 by scientists James Horne and Olov Östberg to quantify a person's time of day preference or "chronotype". The MEQ is still the most widely used chronotype assessment tool in scientific studies today.
