When to Seek Professional Help for a Sleep Problem

Any of the following in an adult warrants a call to your healthcare provider:

  • Not being able to fall asleep or stay asleep at night more than 3 times per week
  • Not being able to fall asleep or stay asleep at night after about 2 weeks of applying the Healthy Sleep Checklist
  • Sleeping at least 7 hours per night but still having difficulty staying awake during the day.
  • Sleeping more than 10 hours per night on a regular basis (pertains only to adults - most children and some adolescents need at least 10 hours per night)
  • Not feeling refreshed after sleep
  • An urge to move your limbs that causes you difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep
  • Bed partner or other tells you that you gasp or stop breathing during your sleep
